Decision Guide: Do You Need to Train the Backbone?

Q1: Is your robot task visually similar to everyday scenes?
Yes (household, tabletop, objects) → Backbone's pre-trained features are likely sufficient.
No (underwater, microscopy, industrial) → Backbone needs adaptation. Consider LoRA or full fine-tune.
Q2: How much demonstration data do you have?
< 1K demos → Action expert only. Training backbone risks overfitting.
1K-10K demos → LoRA is the sweet spot.
> 50K demos → Full fine-tune becomes viable.
Q3: Does the task require language grounding?
Single fixed task ("push the block") → Language grounding doesn't matter much. Expert-only is fine.
Multi-task with varied instructions → LoRA helps the backbone map instructions to actions.
Open-vocabulary tasks → Backbone adaptation is essential.
Q4: What hardware do you have?
MacBook / consumer GPU (≤16GB) → Action expert only.
RTX 4090 / A10 (24GB) → LoRA + expert.
A100 / H100 (40-80GB) → Any strategy works.

The Bottom Line

Start with action expert only. Gemma 4's pre-trained vision and language features are remarkably good for everyday robotic tasks. The backbone already understands objects, spatial relationships, and instructions — the action expert just needs to learn the motor mapping.

Upgrade to LoRA when you have enough data (1K+ demos) and you see the action expert plateauing — especially if tasks require nuanced language grounding or unusual visual domains.

Full fine-tuning is rarely necessary for single-robot setups. It's the pi0 approach for training across dozens of embodiments with millions of trajectories. For research and prototyping, frozen backbone + action expert gets you 80-90% of the way.
